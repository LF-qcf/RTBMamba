# RTBMamba

Borehole strain observations provide minute-level records of crustal deformation, but their analysis is affected by long-term drift, tidal responses, environmental disturbances and station-dependent variability. This article introduces a reproducible RTBMamba-based framework for stable background estimation and residual anomaly analysis in nonstationary borehole strain records. In this framework, raw four-component strain observations are converted into areal strain, low-frequency drift is attenuated, and Robust Tail-aware Bidirectional Mamba is used as the core background estimator for detrended sequences. Deviations between observations and predicted background are then represented as point-level residual indicators and day-level anomaly sequences. We demonstrate the framework using YRY-4 four-component borehole strain observations from four stations associated with the 2022 Maerkang earthquake in Sichuan, China. Synthetic signals with known tidal backgrounds and Maerkang station records show that the framework maintains background continuity during stable intervals while avoiding excessive tracking of local disturbances, leaving anomalous departures distinguishable in the residual series.
